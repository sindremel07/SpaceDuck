Intro to SpaceDuck

Have you ever dreamt of being in the outer space dodging meteors? Or maybe even destroying the meteors with "rema boller"? Well look no further, SpaceDuck is a 2d game where you play as a duck and shoot meteors, if you ever get bored of duck(which is weird, since ducks are sick), you have the option to play as "Henry" or "Oliver"

About the project & me

Hello! My name is Sindre, and I am a 16 year old studying IM at glemmen. SpaceDuck is just a school project that I have worked on since early February, it all started from a small idea where I had 3 main parts. A duck, a timer, and meteors falling from the sky. Since this was my first time ever actually coding something in python, I had to learn ALOT, and trying to code your first ever game with no experience can be a challenge.

That's why I code a litte different... You see most people usually just watch a youtube tutorial or two and copypasting some code, thinking they will learn something from it, in reality they won't. When I first started the project I tried making different functions and playing around with how the language works, and when I understood the basics and how to reuse old code that's when I started on making the actual game. You see, there is nothing wrong with watching a youtube tutorial and searching things up, but if you know the basics and how things work, things will go easier and you will learn quicker, since you know what you are writing. And just like that, you're learning quicker while coding at the same time.

Planning of the Project

1. Getting Started

I began with a simple idea: to create a fun game set in space. I figured out how the game would work and what it would look like. This involved:

• Deciding how the player would move and what challenges they'd face.

• Drawing pictures of the characters, backgrounds, and other things you see in the game.

• Making sure the game could respond when you pressed buttons to move around.

2. Growing Along the Way:

As I worked on the game, I came up with new ideas to make it even better. Here's what I added as I went along:

• I gave the player a way to shoot at enemies, making the game more exciting.

• I added sounds and music to make the game feel more alive.

• I created a system where players could earn points and buy things in the game.

• In Patch v1.2, I reworked the database system, creating a single .db file instead of multiple text files containing information. This streamlined the game's data management and improved efficiency.

3. Making Improvements:

I didn't stop there! I kept making the game better with each update:

• In the first update (Patch v1.0), I made sure all the basic parts of the game were working well.

• In the next update (Patch v1.1), I let players customize their characters and added some new features.

• I kept improving things like how items appear in the game and fixing any problems that popped up.

4. What's Next?

As I look to the future, the journey of Spaceduck continues. Stay tuned for upcoming updates, including new content, optimizations, and community-driven features.

Playtesting & Feedback

• Hva synes du om spillets grafikk og design?

Jeg synes spillets grafikk og design er ganske bra, alt passer sammen og det er klart at det er et verdensrom tema.

• Var det vanskelig å navigere gjennom spillet?

Nei, det var ganske simpelt å navigere gjennom spillet. Siden det var jo ganske klart hva de forskjellige knappene ledet til

• Er det enkelt å starte ett spill?

Ja! Jeg måtte bare trykke på «Spacebar» så kom jeg rett inn i et spill.

• Hvordan styrer du karakteren i spillet, bevegelse og skyting? (hvilke taster, og trenger du mus?)

Du går til sidene med piltastene eller med «A/D» knappene, du skyter bare ved å venstre klikke på musen.

• Hvordan endrer du vanskelighetsgrad?

Du trykker på ikonet med den stjerna i midten, også bare velger du hvilket nivå derifra

• Hvordan skrur du av musikk?

Du trykker på tannhjul ikonet og skrur av musikken der.

• Hvordan endrer du karakter?

Du trykker på den karakter ikonet og endrer der inne.
